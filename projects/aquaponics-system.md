## About

For one of my Electrical Engineering courses, me and a team created an aquaponics sytem with device peripheals to measure the health of the pond. An aquaponics system is a sustainable food production system that combines traditional aquaculture (raising aquatic animals such as fish, crayfish, or shrimp in tanks) with hydroponics (cultivating plants in water). In this system, the waste produced by aquatic animals provides nutrients for plants grown hydroponically, which in turn purify the water for the animals. It's a symbiotic environment where both plants and aquatic animals benefit, creating an efficient, closed-loop system. This method is often praised for its low water usage and its ability to produce both protein (from the fish) and plants in a relatively small space. We wanted to use various sensors to check the water level, temperature and pH levels of the pond. These would also be able to be accessed via a WiFi.

## My responsibilities

I was interested in the programming side of the project. I configured the libraries for measuring the temperature, and water levels using Arduino's IDE. Afterwards, the device was assembled with the arduino microcontroller (ESP32-S3) circuit circuit board.

## Learning Outcome

It was a fun experience. Me and my team frequently met each week to build a progress report and I obtained an understanding off how a engineering project would look in a career. There were some disagreements that some of us wanted to implement within a certain time, but we settled on some changes to fit everyone's needs, such as designing, implementing, and then presenting the project at the end of the semester.
